READING: Impact of AI on Education

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming the educational landscape. Unlike traditional pedagogies which rely on a one-size-fits-all approach, AI-driven platforms can analyze a student's learning patterns and adapt content in real-time. This personalization ensures that learners neither become bored by material that is too easy, nor overwhelmed by concepts that are too difficult.

However, critics argue that the over-reliance on technology may diminish the role of human teachers. While an algorithm can efficiently grade multiple-choice tests and identify mathematical errors, it lacks the emotional intelligence required to mentor a struggling teenager. Furthermore, the implementation of such advanced systems requires significant financial investment, potentially widening the educational gap between wealthy and underfunded schools.

Despite these concerns, early adopters report significant improvements in student engagement. Schools in South Korea and Finland that have integrated AI tutors into their curricula have seen test scores rise by an average of 15% over two years. The consensus among technologists is that AI will not replace teachers, but rather augment their capabilities, freeing them from administrative burdens to focus on high-value interpersonal interactions.

Questions 1-3: True, False, Not Given

1. AI platforms provide the same content to all students regardless of ability.

2. Algorithms possess the emotional capacity to effectively mentor students.

3. Finnish schools spend more money on AI than South Korean schools.

Questions 4-6: Multiple Choice

4. What is a primary concern regarding the cost of AI in schools?

5. How much did test scores increase in early adopting schools?

6. According to technologists, what is the future role of AI for teachers?

Questions 7-8: Fill in the blank (ONE WORD ONLY)

7. AI systems can adapt learning material in real-time to provide to each student.

8. While AI can mark tests, it cannot provide the emotional needed by struggling teenagers.
